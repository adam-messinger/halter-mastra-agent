# Farmer Questions Test Suite

Questions to test the Halter Farm Assistant's ability to handle real farmer queries.

## Overview

- How many cows do I have? How does that compare to last year?
- What mobs do I have?
- How many mobs?
- What are my biggest mobs?
- What is my stocking rate? Is that high or low?
- What's the average age of my cows? Is that too old/young?
- Is my herd good?
- What are my pet cows?
- What cows do I like the most?
- Which cows should I cull?
- Which cows should I dry off early?

## Health

- How many sick cows do I have?
- Do I have any sick cows at the moment?
- What 5 cows have been sick the most?
- What cows are sick the most often? What cows are often sick and also old or low breeding worth?
- How many cows have I lost this season? Is that good or bad?
- What are my best cows?

## Mating

- Is my mating season going well?
- How does it compare to last year, or how does it compare to my neighbors, or how does it compare to my neighbours vs how last year compared to my neighbours?
- How does it compare to industry averages (for system and farm type). What are my opportunities within mating?
- What cows/trends are good/bad?
- What's my cycling rate at the moment, how does it compare, is it good or bad?
- What's my non return rate at the moment, how does it compare, is it good or bad?
- What's my estimated in-calf rate at the moment, how does it compare, is it good or bad?
- Are my cows cycling well after calving?
- How many cows have I excluded from the season? Which cows?
- Have I intervened with many cows?
- How many of my cows did not cycle before the planned start of mating?
- What groups of cows are over performing or under performing for reproduction performance?
- What are the common ways to improve mating performance?
- Does the timing of AI and heat matter? Whens best?
- Did I miss any cows 'on heat' this morning?

## Farm System

- What is the primary goal of my dairy system: profit per hectare, profit per cow, or lifestyle and workload balance?
- Should my farm run as all-grass, grass plus supplements, or a higher-input system given my soils and climate?
- What stocking rate can my farm realistically carry in an average season without buying excessive feed?
- How does my optimal stocking rate change in a wet year, a dry year, or under irrigation?
- Am I better off running more moderate cows at higher stocking rate, or fewer high-producing cows with more inputs?
- What is my realistic target for milksolids per hectare and per cow in this district?
- Is my current calving pattern (spring/autumn/split) still the best fit for my milk contract and pasture growth curve?
- How dependent is my system on purchased feed, and am I comfortable with that exposure to price and supply shocks?
- What are the biggest constraints on production on this farm: pasture growth, cow condition, labour, shed capacity, or cash?
- If I changed nothing else, which single constraint would unlock the most extra profit or resilience?

## Genetics and Breeding

- What is the ideal cow type for this farm: Friesian, Jersey, crossbred, or another mix, given my pasture and payout structure?
- Are my cows too big and high-maintenance for a largely grass-based system, or too small to take advantage of my feed supply?
- How much emphasis should I put on fertility vs production vs longevity in breeding decisions?
- Am I using BW, PW, and reliability figures effectively when selecting bulls and replacements?
- How much inbreeding risk do I have in the herd, and how am I managing it?
- Should I be using more sexed semen, beef semen, or both to improve herd genetics and calf value?
- Are my top cows truly my top profit cows, or just my highest-volume producers?
- What physical faults (feet, udders, temperament) are costing me most in lost milk and culling?
- Am I better to breed for slightly less peak production but better fertility and robustness under pasture-only feeding?
- How should I balance using AI sires vs natural bulls to hit my breeding and workload goals?

## Pasture Management

- What does my pasture growth curve actually look like month by month, not just what I assume?
- How accurate is my pasture measurement (plate meter, eyeballing, software) and do I use that data for decisions?
- What is my true average pasture utilisation: how much grown vs how much eaten or wasted?
- Am I carrying too much pasture into spring or too little into summer and autumn?
- Do I have clear target pre-graze and post-graze covers for each time of the season?
- How often do my actual residuals match those targets, and what stops me when they do not?
- Is my regrassing programme fast enough to keep the proportion of young, productive pasture high?
- Are my pasture species (ryegrass, clover, herbs) still the best fit for my soils and climate trends?
- Would alternative forages (fodder beet, lucerne, kale, plantain mixes) improve my feed supply or just add complexity?
- How often am I short of pasture for more than a week or two, and what patterns cause that shortage?
- Is my rotation length set by plant needs, cow needs, or habit?
- How quickly do I adjust rotation length in response to changes in growth rate and forecast?
- Am I using on-off grazing, back-fencing, or other tools to protect pasture in wet conditions?
- Where are my worst-performing paddocks, and do I know exactly why they underperform (drainage, fertility, species, aspect)?
- Is soil fertility (pH, P, K, S, trace elements) limiting pasture growth, and do I have recent soil tests?
- How efficiently am I using nitrogen: timing, rate, and placement versus leaching and waste?
- Could better pasture management reduce my reliance on bought-in feed by a meaningful amount?
- Do I know the real cost per tonne of DM of my home-grown pasture versus purchased feeds?
- Are my laneways, water troughs, and paddock layouts helping or hindering even pasture utilisation?
- Am I leaving enough residual leaf area to maintain pasture quality and persistence over years?

## Reproduction

- What is my current 6-week in-calf rate, and what is realistically achievable on this farm?
- How many days after calving are cows typically cycling and ready to mate?
- Are my cows going to mating with an adequate body condition score to conceive early?
- Is my mating period too long, causing a drawn-out calving and extra labour, or too short for my current management?
- How effectively am I detecting heats: visual observation, tail paint, activity monitors, or a mix?
- Do I have a clear plan for anoestrous cows before and during mating (metrics, interventions, vet input)?
- What proportion of herd is mated in the first 3 weeks, and how does that affect my calving profile and production?
- Should I use synchrony programmes in heifers or cows to tighten mating and calving, and does the economics stack up?
- What is my empty rate and what are the key drivers: nutrition, genetics, disease, or mating management?
- How am I using mating and culling decisions to steadily improve herd fertility over several seasons?

## Calving

- Is my planned start of calving aligned with when I will have enough cheap feed for freshly calved cows?
- Are cows at target condition score at drying off and at calving, and how often do I check this objectively?
- How well am I managing the transition period from dry cow to early lactation to minimise metabolic disease?
- Do I have clear protocols for down cows, milk fever, ketosis, and other peripartum issues?
- Is my calving area set up to minimise stress, mud, and disease pressure for cows and calves?
- How often do cows need calving assistance, and what can I change to reduce this?
- Do I intervene too early or too late in difficult calvings, based on vet guidance?
- How am I ensuring newborn calves get adequate, good-quality colostrum quickly after birth?
- What is my actual calf mortality from birth to weaning, and what are the main causes?
- Could changing calving date, cow genetics, or body condition materially reduce calving problems?

## Replacements and Youngstock

- How many replacements do I actually need given my cull rate and expansion or contraction plans?
- Am I rearing too many or too few heifers relative to the herd I want in 3â€“5 years?
- What are my criteria for selecting heifer calves: genetics, dam performance, birth date, health?
- Is my colostrum management for replacement calves good enough to protect future herd health and longevity?
- Are rearing facilities (pens, hutches, sheds) giving calves a clean, dry, well-ventilated environment?
- What growth targets do I have for calves and yearlings, and do I measure if they are being hit?
- Are my youngstock grazing and parasite programmes setting them up to calve at the right size and age?
- Should I contract graze heifers or rear them on-farm, and which option gives better long-term results?
- Are my heifers entering the herd in good condition and calving early, or always playing catch-up?
- How do I cull or reclassify poor-performing young cows quickly enough to avoid dragging the herd down?

## Herd Health

- What are the main health issues in my herd: lameness, mastitis, metabolic disease, reproduction, parasites, or others?
- Is my herd health plan genuinely preventative or mostly reactive to problems as they arise?
- Do I have an agreed annual animal health plan with my vet that includes targets and review points?
- How serious is lameness on my farm, and what are the underlying causes: tracks, time on concrete, nutrition, or genetics?
- How effective is my mastitis control: SCC trends, clinical case rate, cure rates, and culling for udder health?
- Am I using dry-cow therapy and teat sealants in a targeted and responsible way?
- Are my parasite control strategies for cows and youngstock based on risk and testing, or just routine treatments?
- How are my handling facilities and milking routine affecting cow stress and injury rates?
- Do I have robust protocols for euthanasia and humane decision-making for unproductive or suffering animals?
- How do I monitor and improve overall animal welfare, not just treat obvious disease?

## Milking and Shed

- Is the milking routine efficient and consistent, or does it change with staff and season?
- Are cups-on times, plant settings, and milking speed matched to cow comfort and teat health?
- How often do I review milking machine performance and maintenance with a technician?
- Is my bulk milk SCC and bactoscan where it needs to be to meet contract and premium standards?
- Are there patterns in mastitis and SCC that point to specific shed practices or equipment issues?
- How well do I and staff follow hygiene protocols in the pit, especially under pressure or fatigue?
- Does the shed layout, yard size, and backing gate operation minimise cow stress and slipping?
- Is shed and effluent infrastructure adequate for the current herd size and any planned growth?
- Could automations (cup removers, drafting, activity collars) pay their way in labour savings or better decisions?
- How could I reduce time per milking while maintaining or improving milk quality and animal welfare?

## Environment and Compliance

- How close am I to current and upcoming NZ environmental regulations on nitrogen loss, waterways, and wintering?
- Where are the main environmental risk areas on my farm: critical source areas, waterways, steeper slopes, or heavy soils?
- How do my grazing and effluent practices affect water quality risk, and what changes would give the biggest gains?
- Is my nitrogen use, stocking rate, and bought-in feed level sustainable under likely future rules and costs?
- Are there practical options to reduce footprint (for example, lower N use, stand-off pads, altered stocking) without killing profitability?
- Do I have good nutrient and effluent management plans that I actually follow day-to-day?

## Labour and Finance

- Is my labour setup sustainable: hours, rosters, housing, and training for me, family, and staff?
- Which tasks could be systematised or delegated to reduce reliance on a few key people?
- What financial metrics do I track regularly (for example, EBIT per hectare, cost of production per kg MS) to guide decisions?
- If milk price dropped sharply or a drought hit next season, what is my pre-planned response for feed, stocking rate, and debt?
